VOLCANIC ERUPTION

WHAT IS VOLCANIC ERUPTION?

A volcanic eruption occurs when magma (molten rock beneath the Earth's surface) rises through a vent or fissure in the Earth's crust. This process is often driven by the buildup of pressure from dissolved gases within the magma, similar to how gas escapes from a shaken soda bottle when opened. As the magma ascends, the pressure decreases, allowing gases to form bubbles, which can lead to explosive eruptions or gentle lava flows depending on the magma's composition and gas content.

SEISMIC ACTIVITY

One of the most common indicators of an impending volcanic eruption is an increase in seismic activity. As magma rises toward the surface, it can cause small earthquakes, known as volcanic tremors. Scientists monitor these tremors using seismographs, and a significant increase in their frequency and intensity can signal that an eruption may be imminent.
