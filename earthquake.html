EARTHQUAKE

WHAT IS EARTHQUAKE?

An earthquake is the sudden shaking or vibration of the ground caused by the movement of tectonic plates beneath the Earth's surface. These movements release energy that travels through the ground, creating seismic waves. Earthquakes can cause damage to buildings, roads, and infrastructure and may trigger landslides, tsunamis, and aftershocks.

INTENSITY VS. MAGNITUDE

Magnitude tells us how much energy an earthquake releases at its origin. Intensity describes how strongly the ground shakes in different areas and how it affects people, structures, and the environment.
